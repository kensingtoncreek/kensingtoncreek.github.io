    Committees

    • Advisory Committee  The primary responsibility of board members is to preserve and maintain the assets of their association. While the advisory committee is not a Board of Directors until duly elected by the homeowners, this is the initial committee that is set up prior to transition. This group of individuals may go on to become the Board of Directors. Its primary responsibility should also be to preserve and maintain the assets of the association. Other responsibilities include identifying transition issues, being a liaison between the community and the management company, working on identifying issues in the community for the HOA to address, reviewing the budget, overseeing the community financials, and offering advise to the man agement company in regard to these areas. They review bids and make recommendations to the management company for services in the community. They also oversee the other committees in the community. This is not the elected Board of Directors but it serves many of the same capacities as a Board, only in an advisory role.

    • Architectural Control Committee  The members of this committee are generally selected by the Board of Directors. The number of members, their duties, and their authority is outlined in the Covenants for the community. This committee generally oversees the “use restrictions” and the aesthetic standards for the community. They review and approve/deny modification requests for the community and work with the Board to establish the guidelines or standards for the community. They assist the Community Manager in identifying violations of the covenants and standards in the community.

    • Recreation Committee  These are volunteers who are interested in the recreation areas of the community. Depending on the amenities in the community, these areas could include the pool, tennis courts, playground, clubhouse, basketball goal, and lake. Persons on this committee are responsible for establishing use rules for these amenities and enforcing them. This committee will obtain bids for regular pool maintenance and identify areas of repair to the management company. They also make recommendations to the Board for financing for these areas and advise the Board members or the management company of repairs that may be required.

    • Social Committee  These are volunteers who are interested in the recreation areas of the community. Depending on the amenities in the community, these areas could include the pool, tennis courts, playground, clubhouse, basketball goal, and lake. Persons on this committee are responsible for establishing use rules for these amenities and enforcing them. This committee will obtain bids for regular pool maintenance and identify areas of repair to the management company. They also make recommendations to the Board for financing for these areas and advise the Board members or the management company of repairs that may be required.

    • Welcome Committee Neighbors should always be the first ones to welcome newcomers to the community—even if that just means knocking on the door, saying hello, and pointing out your house across the street. Members of this committee do just that- they welcome new neighbors to the community, offering them a glimpse of the community and giving the newcomers a positive first impression of the association. Many times, and budget permitting, this committee will prepare a welcome packet and a small gift to take to the new homeowner. This committee also assists in enlisting volunteers as they greet the new neighbors, as they are often anxious to get involved in the community

    • Communication Committee  A newsletter is typically a board's best tool for communicating and promoting association projects. Message boards are great for posting last minute notes and/or reminders. Neighborhood directories are a great way to help communities know how to contact each other. Those are just a few of the things that this committee can do. This committee will work with the advisory committee or the Board of Directors and the management company to write a regular newsletter publication for distribution to the community. This committee may be asked to help with other forms of communication within the community but its primary responsibility will be the newsletter.

    • Landscaping Committee  The community landscaping is one of the community’s most valuable assets that can either have a positive or negative impact on the property values in the community. The landscaping committee can emphasize the need for established standards and explain why varying from them will hurt the community's appearance. A landscape committee should review long-range needs and plan for non-seasonal budget items, such as emergency repairs and plant replacements. This committee will obtain annual bids (or as directed) for landscaping services in the community and will make recommendations to the advisory board or Board of Directors for contracted landscape services. This committee will identify and suggest areas of improve ment and/or beautification. This committee will also be responsible for turning in a budget proposal to the Board of Directors each year.
